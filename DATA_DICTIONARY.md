# Data Dictionary — Rainfall Trend Analysis
## Phetchaburi–Prachuap Khiri Khan River Basin, Western Thailand, 1981–2014

**Pipeline versions:** v3 (`rainfall_trend_analysis_v3.py`) and v4 (`rainfall_trend_analysis_v4.py` + `rta/` package)  
**Analysis period:** 1981–2014 (34 calendar years; 33 complete dry-season hydrological years)  
**Stations:** 12 rain gauges (IDs 500001–500301)  
**Methods:** Standard MK, Modified MK (Hamed & Rao 1998), PW-MK (Yue & Wang 2004), TFPW-MK (Yue et al. 2002)  
**Temporal scales:** Annual (Jan–Dec), Wet Season (May–Oct), Dry Season (Nov–Apr)

## 3. Statistical Result Fields — Standard Mann-Kendall

All Standard MK fields are produced by `standard_mk()` in `rta/trend_tests.py`. The test does **not** correct for serial autocorrelation; use MMK when lag-1 autocorrelation is significant.

| Variable | Description | Units | Source Module | Processing Stage | Output Location |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `MK_S` (column header: `S` in Excel) | Mann-Kendall S statistic: the signed sum of all pairwise comparisons. For a series x of length n, S = Σᵢ₌₁ⁿ⁻¹ Σⱼ₌ᵢ₊₁ⁿ sgn(xⱼ − xᵢ), where sgn returns +1, 0, or −1. S > 0 indicates more concordant than discordant pairs (increasing trend); S < 0 indicates decreasing trend; S near 0 suggests no trend. Implemented with a fast loop in `_mk_s_fast()`. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_tests.py` `_mk_s_fast()`, called inside `standard_mk()` | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S1 (column "S"), WB2:01, WB2:11 |
| `Var_S` (column header: `Var(S)` in Excel) | Variance of S under the null hypothesis of no trend, with tie correction. Formula: Var(S) = [n(n−1)(2n+5) − Σₜ t(t−1)(2t+5)] / 18, where the sum runs over groups of tied values of size t. Computed in `mk_variance_ties()`. A larger Var(S) produces a smaller Z and larger p-value. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_tests.py` `mk_variance_ties()` | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S1 (column "Var(S)"), WB2:01, WB2:11 |
| `MK_Z` (column header: `Z` in Excel) | Mann-Kendall Z test statistic: Z = (S − 1)/√Var(S) if S > 0, Z = (S + 1)/√Var(S) if S < 0, Z = 0 if S = 0 (continuity correction applied). Follows a standard normal distribution under H₀. |Z| > 1.960 → significant at α = 0.05; |Z| > 2.5758 → significant at α = 0.01. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_tests.py` `standard_mk()` line 122–123 | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S1, WB1:S3 (as "MK Z"), WB1:S7 (as "MK_Z"), WB2:01, WB2:11, WB2:12, WB2:13, WB2:90 |
| `MK_p` (column header: `p-value` in Excel) | Two-tailed p-value: p = 2 × [1 − Φ(|Z|)], where Φ is the standard normal CDF from `scipy.stats.norm.cdf`. Capped at 1.0. Values below 0.05 indicate a statistically significant trend at the 5% level. | dimensionless (probability) | `rta/trend_tests.py` `standard_mk()` line 124 | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S1 (as "p-value"), WB1:S3 (as "MK p"), WB1:S7 (as "MK_p"), WB2:01, WB2:11, WB2:12, WB2:13, WB2:90 |
| `tau` (Kendall's τ) | Kendall's rank correlation coefficient: τ = S / [n(n−1)/2]. Ranges from −1 (perfect negative) to +1 (perfect positive). Provides a scale-independent measure of trend strength. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_tests.py` `standard_mk()` line 125 | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S1 (as "τ (Kendall)"), WB2:01 (as "Tau") |
| `MK_sig` (column header: `* p<0.05` or `** p<0.01` in Excel) | Significance label string. Values: `"**"` when p < 0.01, `"*"` when 0.01 ≤ p < 0.05, `"ns"` when p ≥ 0.05. Produced in `rta/batch.py` `_sig_label()`. | – | `rta/batch.py` `_sig_label()` | Stage 4–5: labelling | WB1:S1 (two columns: "* p<0.05" and "** p<0.01"), WB1:S7 (as "MK_sig"), WB2:01 (as "Sig_label_05"/"Sig_label_01"), WB2:90 (as "MK_sig") |
| `MK_sig_05` (column header: `Sig_p05` or `Sig_Z05` in WB2) | Boolean flag: `True` when MK p-value < 0.05 (p-based) or when |MK_Z| ≥ 1.960 (Z-based). ## 5. Statistical Result Fields — Modified Mann-Kendall (Hamed & Rao 1998)

The Modified MK test shares S, Var_S, tau, slope_Q, slope_lo, and slope_hi with the Standard MK (same input series). It differs by computing an autocorrelation-corrected variance Var*(S), an effective sample size n*, and a corrected Z and p.

| Variable | Description | Units | Source Module | Processing Stage | Output Location |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `rho_1` | Lag-1 Pearson autocorrelation coefficient of the **original** annual (or seasonal) series: r₁ = Σᵢ(xᵢ − x̄)(xᵢ₊₁ − x̄) / Σᵢ(xᵢ − x̄)². Computed by `lag_k_autocorr(x, k=1)` in `rta/autocorr.py`. Used to diagnose serial dependence before choosing between MK and MMK. | dimensionless | `rta/autocorr.py` `lag_k_autocorr()`; assembled in `rta/batch.py` `run_all()` | Stage 3: autocorrelation | WB1:S1 (as "rho_1"), WB1:S3 (as "ρ₁"), WB2:01 (as "rho_1"), WB2:11, WB2:90 |
| `Sig_AC` | Significant autocorrelation flag. Two-tailed test: |r₁| > z₀.₀₂₅ / √n = 1.960 / √n. Computed by `is_sig_autocorr()` in `rta/autocorr.py`. Stored as a Boolean in internal DataFrames; written as `"Yes*"` or `"No"` in Excel (S3, S7). When `Sig_AC = True`, the MK test may overestimate significance; MMK or PW/TFPW corrections are recommended. | – | `rta/autocorr.py` `is_sig_autocorr()`; `rta/batch.py` `run_all()` | Stage 3: autocorrelation | WB1:S3, WB1:S7, WB2:01–04, WB2:11–13, WB2:90 |
| `rho_1_ranked` (column header: `ρ₁` in WB1:S2) | Lag-1 autocorrelation of the **ranked** series used in the MMK variance correction. Hamed & Rao (1998) use ranks to ensure the AC estimate is robust to the distribution of the original series. Computed via `scipy.stats.rankdata(x)` then `all_lag_autocorr()`. This value may differ from `rho_1` of the original series. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_tests.py` `modified_mk()` lines 177–178 | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S2 (as "ρ₁"), WB2:02 (as "rho_1_ranked") |
| `Var_S_adj` (column header: `Var*(S)` in Excel) | Autocorrelation-corrected variance of S: Var*(S) = Var(S) × (n / n*). The inflation factor n/n* is computed from only the statistically significant ranked-series autocorrelations (those with |ρₖ| > z₀.₀₂₅ / √n). Using only significant lags follows Hamed & Rao (1998) strictly and avoids overcorrection when most lags are near zero. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_tests.py` `modified_mk()` line 192 | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S2 (as "Var*(S)"), WB2:02, WB2:11 |
| `n_eff` (column header: `n_eff` in Excel) | Effective sample size: n* = n / [1 + (2/n) × Σₖ₌₁ᵐ (n − k) ρₖ*], where ρₖ* is ρₖ of the ranked series if significant, else 0. The max lag m = min(n//3, n−1). When there is no significant autocorrelation, n* = n and no correction is applied. When positive autocorrelation is present, n* < n (fewer effective observations than actual years), leading to larger Var*(S) and a smaller (more conservative) |Z|. | years | `rta/trend_tests.py` `modified_mk()` line 191 | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S2 (as "n_eff"), WB2:02, WB2:11, WB2:90 |
| `Correction_Factor` | Variance inflation factor CF = Var*(S) / Var(S) = n / n*. CF = 1.0 means no autocorrelation correction was needed. CF > 1.0 means positive autocorrelation inflated the apparent significance of the MK test; the correction reduces |Z|. CF < 1.0 (possible but rare, caused by significant negative autocorrelation) means the correction increases |Z|. Computed in `rta/trend_method_comparison.py` `_ac_block()` as `Var*(S) / Var(S)` from WB1:S2. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_method_comparison.py` `_ac_block()` | Stage 5: post-processing | WB2:02, WB2:11, WB2:12, WB2:13, WB2:90 |
| `Lag_parsed` (column header: `Lag` in WB2) | Human-readable string listing the significant lag-k autocorrelations used in the MMK variance correction, formatted as `"k=1(ρ=+0.471); k=9(ρ=−0.494)"`, or `"—"` if none were significant. Sourced from the supplementary workbook (WB4, sheet "3_Modified_MK", column "Significant_Lags") and parsed by `_parse_lags()` in `rta/trend_method_comparison.py`. Returns `"—"` if the supplementary workbook was not provided. | – | `rta/trend_method_comparison.py` `_parse_lags()` | Stage 5: post-processing | WB2:02 (as "Lag"), WB2:11, WB2:12, WB2:13, WB2:90 |
| `MMK_Z` | Modified MK Z-statistic with autocorrelation-corrected variance: Z* = (S − 1)/√Var*(S) if S > 0, (S + 1)/√Var*(S) if S < 0, 0 if S = 0. Smaller in absolute value than MK_Z when positive autocorrelation is present. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_tests.py` `modified_mk()` lines 194–195 | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S2 (as "Z"), WB1:S3 (as "MMK Z"), WB1:S7 (as "MMK_Z"), WB2:02, WB2:11, WB2:90 |

## 7. Statistical Result Fields — Trend-Free PW-MK (TFPW-MK, Yue et al. 2002)

TFPW-MK is only available in the v4 pipeline (`rta/tfpw.py` and `rta/trend_tests.py`). The algorithm avoids the bias introduced by standard prewhitening when a real trend is present.

| Variable | Description | Units | Source Module | Processing Stage | Output Location |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `TFPW_Z` | Z-statistic from TFPW-MK. Algorithm: (1) estimate Sen's slope β of original series; (2) detrend: x_d[i] = x[i] − β × (i+1); (3) compute ρ₁ of x_d; (4a) if ρ₁ not significant, run MK on original x; (4b) if ρ₁ significant, prewhiten x_d: y[i] = x_d[i+1] − ρ₁ × x_d[i], restore trend: z[i] = y[i] + β × (i+2), run MK on z. The restored trend ensures the slope estimate is not attenuated by prewhitening. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_tests.py` `tfpw_mk()` (also `rta/tfpw.py` `tfpw_mk()`) | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S7 (as "TFPW_Z"), WB2:04, WB2:90 |
| `TFPW_p` | Two-tailed p-value for TFPW-MK. | dimensionless | `rta/trend_tests.py` `tfpw_mk()` | Stage 4: trend testing | WB1:S7 (as "TFPW_p"), WB2:04, WB2:90 |
| `beta_initial` | Sen's slope β of the **original** (pre-detrending) series, used in step 1 of the TFPW algorithm. Stored for diagnostic purposes to verify that trend removal was applied correctly. | mm/yr | `rta/tfpw.py` `tfpw_mk()` | Stage 4: trend testing | Internal DataFrames only |

## 10. Field Significance Fields

Field significance tests whether the number of locally significant stations exceeds what would be expected by chance, accounting for the multiple-testing problem across 12 spatially correlated stations.

| Variable | Description | Units | Source Module | Processing Stage | Output Location |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `Walker_p` (column headers: `Walker_p_MK`, `Walker_p_MMK`) | Walker (1914) binomial field significance p-value. Under H₀ (no field-wide trend), the count of significant stations follows Binomial(n, α). The one-sided p-value is P(X ≥ n_sig | n=N_stations, p=α) = 1 − Binomial_CDF(n_sig − 1, N_stations, α), computed via `scipy.stats.binom.sf`. Reported separately for MK and MMK significance counts. | dimensionless | `rta/field_significance.py` `walker_test()` | Stage 6: field significance | WB1:S8 (as "Walker_p_MK"), WB2:34 |
| `Walker_sig` (column headers: `Walker_sig_MK`, `Walker_sig_MMK`) | Boolean: `True` if Walker_p < α (0.05). Written as `"Yes*"` or `"No"` in Excel. | – | `rta/field_significance.py` `walker_test()` | Stage 6: field significance | WB1:S8, WB2:34 |
| `LC_p` (column header: `LC_p_MK`) | Livezey-Chen (1983) Monte Carlo field significance p-value. Algorithm: (1) compute S_obs = fraction of stations with Standard MK p < α; (2) for each of 1,000 permutations (random seed 42), independently shuffle each station's series, run MK on each, record the fraction with p < α; (3) p_field_LC = fraction of MC draws ≥ S_obs, floored at 1/n_perm = 0.001. A small p_field_LC indicates that the observed fraction of significant stations is unlikely under the null hypothesis of no trend anywhere in the field. | dimensionless | `rta/field_significance.py` `livezey_chen_mc()` | Stage 6: field significance | WB1:S8 (as "LC_p_MK"), WB2:34 |
| `LC_sig` (column header: `LC_sig_MK`) | Boolean: `True` if LC_p < α. | – | `rta/field_significance.py` `livezey_chen_mc()` | Stage 6: field significance | WB1:S8, WB2:34 |
| `LC_null_mean` | Mean of the Monte Carlo null distribution (mean fraction of significant stations under random permutation). Should be close to α = 0.05 for large n_perm; deviations indicate spatial correlation among stations. | dimensionless | `rta/field_significance.py` `livezey_chen_mc()` | Stage 6: field significance | WB1:S8, WB2:34 |
| `LC_null_95th` | 95th percentile of the MC null distribution. The observed S_obs must exceed this value for the result to be field-significant at the 5% level. | dimensionless | `rta/field_significance.py` `livezey_chen_mc()` | Stage 6: field significance | WB1:S8, WB2:34 |
| `S_obs` | Observed fraction of stations with p < α under the Standard MK test. S_obs = n_sig_obs / N_stations. | dimensionless | `rta/field_significance.py` `livezey_chen_mc()` | Stage 6: field significance | Internal DataFrames; not written to Excel |
| `N_sig_MK` / `N_sig_MMK` / `N_sig_PW` / `N_sig_TFPW` | Count of stations at each temporal scale where the respective method gives p < 0.05. Used as input to Walker test and as summary statistics. | dimensionless | `rta/field_significance.py` `field_sig_summary()`; extended in `rta/trend_method_comparison.py` `build_34_field_significance()` | Stage 6: field significance | WB1:S8, WB2:34 |
| `Frac_sig_MK` / `Frac_sig_MMK` / `Frac_sig_PW` / `Frac_sig_TFPW` | Fraction of stations significant (N_sig / N_stations) per method per scale. | dimensionless | `rta/field_significance.py`; `rta/trend_method_comparison.py` | Stage 6: field significance | WB1:S8, WB2:34 |
| `N_stations` | Total number of stations with sufficient data (N ≥ 10 years) at a given temporal scale. | dimensionless | `rta/field_significance.py` `field_sig_summary()` | Stage 6: field significance | WB1:S8, WB2:34, WB2:35 |

## 14. Temporal Aggregation Rules

These rules govern which daily values contribute to each temporal scale. They affect both the N field (completeness counts) and the quality of the statistical time series.

| Scale | Months included | Completeness threshold | Hydrological year note | Typical N per station |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Annual (Jan–Dec) | January–December (calendar year) | ≥ 80% of days present in the year | No shift; each calendar year is labelled by its own year integer | 34 (1981–2014) |
| Wet Season (May–Oct) | May, June, July, August, September, October | ≥ 80% of days present in the 6-month window | No shift; labelled by calendar year | 34 |
| Dry Season (Nov–Apr) | November, December (year Y), January, February, March, April (year Y+1) | ≥ 80% of days present in the 6-month block | November and December of year Y are date-shifted to year Y+1, so the label "Y+1" spans Nov Y → Apr Y+1. Boundary years at the start (1982: Jan–Apr only) and end (2014: Nov–Dec only) are excluded because they do not contain all 6 required months. | 33 (1982–2014) |
| Monthly (monthly_all) | Each calendar month individually | ≥ 80% of days present in the month | No shift | Up to 408 monthly values (34 × 12) |

A year-scale value is `NaN` if fewer than the completeness threshold of days are non-missing. `NaN` years are excluded from MK test inputs via `.dropna()`.
